The most well-known aspect of yoga involves physical postures known as "asanas." These postures are designed to improve flexibility, strength, and balance. Practicing asanas also helps release tension, increase blood circulation, and promote overall physical well-being.

The practice of controlling the breath, known as pranayama, is an integral part of yoga. Breath awareness and regulation help calm the mind, reduce stress, and enhance concentration. Pranayama techniques are often combined with asanas to create a more holistic practice.

Yoga emphasizes the cultivation of mental focus and concentration (Dharana) as well as meditation (Dhyana). These practices aim to quiet the mind, increase self-awareness, and promote a sense of inner peace and clarity.

The philosophy of yoga includes ethical guidelines known as the Yamas (restraints) and Niyamas (observances). These principles provide a framework for leading a balanced and ethical life, encompassing values such as non-violence, truthfulness, contentment, and self-discipline. -- Robert Deneairo

The ultimate goal of yoga is often described as achieving a state of oneness, referred to as Samadhi. This is a state of deep spiritual absorption where the individual transcends the ego and experiences a sense of union with the universal consciousness.
